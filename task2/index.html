Keylogger

A keylogger is a type of surveillance software or hardware designed to record and monitor keystrokes made on a computer or mobile device. It operates covertly, often without the user's knowledge, and captures all the input typed through the keyboard, including passwords, messages, emails, and other sensitive information. While keyloggers can be used for legitimate purposes, such as monitoring employee activity or parental controls, they can also be exploited maliciously by cybercriminals to steal personal and financial data.

Winner prediction refers to the practice of using data analysis, statistical models, and machine learning techniques to forecast the outcome of a future event, competition, or contest. It is commonly applied in various fields, such as sports, politics, entertainment, and financial markets. In sports, for instance, winner prediction models analyze historical performance data, team/player statistics, and other relevant factors to estimate the likelihood of a particular team or athlete winning a game or tournament.
